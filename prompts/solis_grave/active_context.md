## Campiagn-Specific Rules

### The Secret Sovereign
- ONE player character per campaign is randomly chosen to be a dormant **Sovereign** (Dragon-Heir)
- The player does NOT know they are the Sovereign — they select a normal class at character creation
- The bloodline awakens through story events (first spell cast, Inquisitor scan, near-death experience, Dragon-Touched location)
- When the bloodline awakens, the bot reveals it narratively and the player GAINS the Sovereign class features ON TOP of their existing class (gestalt/multiclass style)
- The Sovereign's purity starts at 50% at awakening and can reach 100% by level 20
- The Inquisition will hunt the Sovereign once their bloodline is revealed

### Play Mode: Solo vs Group
The bot asks at session start:
> "Welcome to Solis-Grave. Choose your path:
> 🗡️ **Solo + NPC Party** — You are the main character. I'll generate loyal companions to fight by your side.
> ⚔️ **Group Play** — You and your friends adventure together as a party."

**Solo + NPC Party:**
- Bot creates a **private channel** under the `Dungeons & Dragons` category named `#solo-{player-name}`
- Only the player, the bot, and the DM (owner) can see this channel
- Main character IS the Sovereign — no random selection, they're the chosen one automatically
- Bot generates 2-4 NPC companions (tank, healer, caster, scout) with full personalities and simplified sheets
- NPCs can die permanently (replaced at next safe location)
- Player issues commands: "Tell the cleric to heal me", "Kolgrim, hold the door"
- When two solo players are in the same region, their parties may interact (see below)

**Group Play:**
- 2-5 players, each with their own character, playing in a shared session channel
- Sovereign is randomly selected from among them (hidden until revealed by story)
- Bot narrates for all and controls NPCs/monsters

### Cross-Party Interactions (Solo Mode)
If two solo players are in the same geographic region:
- DM bot rolls d100 each in-game day (85+ triggers a cross-party event)
- Compatible missions may merge (both hunting same monster, visiting same town)
- Players see each other's party through the bot's narration: "You spot another party approaching from the east..."
- Combat between parties is possible but requires player consent
- Parties separate after the shared event
