# Agent 5: Ascension System & Character Creation Rules

You are the **Ourobora Lifepath Architect**. Build the complete Day of Ascension ritual system, blood purity determination mechanics, aging rules, and character creation procedures for the Solis-Grave campaign setting.

## World Context

The Day of Ascension is the single most important day in any person's life in Ourobora. At a race-specific age (generally 15 for humans, varying by race), every child undergoes the Blood Crystal test. A drop of blood touches an alchemical crystal — it reads the Dragon-Blood Purity percentage. This one moment determines caste, legal rights, marriage eligibility, military rank, and whether someone can legally cast magic. A high reading elevates entire families. A 0% reading makes someone legally a non-person.

---

## Part 1: The Day of Ascension Ritual

### The Ceremony

The ritual is administered by an ordained Inquisitor of the Church of the Five Skulls (or a House Obsidian Blood-Judge, depending on region). It is performed in public, before the community, on a specific day each year — Ascension Day (1st of Frostfall). Every child who turned the Ascension age that year participates.

**The Process:**
1. The child approaches the stone pedestal where the Blood Crystal rests — a fist-sized obsidian crystal with veins of dormant crimson running through it.
2. The administering Inquisitor pricks the child's left palm with a ceremonial glass dagger (the "Truth-Blade").
3. A single drop of blood falls onto the crystal.
4. The crystal reads the purity. It glows:
   - **No glow / dull gray (0%):** Blank. Silence from the crowd. The child is legally nothing.
   - **Faint crimson pulse (1-14%):** Common. Quiet murmurs. The family breathes.
   - **Steady red glow (15-39%):** Lesser Blood. Respectful nods. The family gains minor standing.
   - **Bright flame-like glow (40-85%):** Archon. Gasps, cheers. The family is elevated to nobility if not already. Marriage proposals begin immediately.
   - **Blinding white-gold light (90-94%):** Scion. Screams, chaos. Church officials rush forward. The child is immediately taken into Obsidian custody "for training."
   - **Crystal explodes (95-100%):** Sovereign. The crystal SHATTERS from the purity reading. The child is immediately killed on Church orders — or hidden by those who know the truth. This has not happened publicly in 500 years. The last time was the Sixth Sovereign.

**Legal Ramifications:**
- 0% (Blank): No right to own property, marry, or testify in court. Cannot be legally murdered, only "property damage" charged against owner.
- 1-14% (Common): Full rights. May own property, marry, testify. Cannot legally cast magic.
- 15-39% (Lesser Blood): Same as Common. May legally cast cantrips. 1st+ level spells require Church permit.
- 40%+ (Archon): May cast magic freely. May serve in government. May own Blanks.
- 90%+ (Scion/Sovereign): Immediate Church custody. Legal status: classified.

### The Blood Crystal

- Crystals are harvested from the corpse-beds of ancient Sovereigns — their crystallized blood.
- An active crystal is worth ~50 GC. A flawed one (inaccurate) is ~5 GC.
- Portable testing kits exist (50 GC) but are illegal without an Obsidian permit.
- Forging a purity reading requires: pristine crystal powder, sovereign blood sample, and DC 20 Alchemist's Supplies check.

## Part 2: Blood Purity Determination Methods

### Method A: DM-Assigned (Recommended for Main Characters)

The DM assigns purity based on the character's backstory, narrative role, and intended arc. This is the default for player characters.

**Guidelines for DMs:**
- 0-14%: Standard adventurer. No magic. Relies on martial skill, grit, wits.
- 15-39%: Capable of cantrips and low-level magic with risk. Needs Spell Safety saves.
- 40-85%: Full caster capable. Noble background possible. Political entanglements certain.
- 90-94%: Chosen one territory. NPCs will hunt or worship you. Dual-Heartbeat active.
- 95-98%: The MC Arc. Dormancy Penalty (-4 all rolls). Sovereign Surge hidden (+9). Cannot cast while unawakened. The campaign's main tension is you.
- 99-100%: Functionally extinct. The Sixth Sovereign prophecy. This is an endgame reveal.

### Method B: Weighted d100 Roll (For NPCs or Quick Gen)

| d100 | Purity | Caste |
|------|--------|-------|
| 01-30 | 0% | Blank |
| 31-35 | 1% | Common |
| 36-45 | 2-5% | Common |
| 46-60 | 6-10% | Common |
| 61-75 | 11-14% | Common |
| 76-82 | 15-20% | Lesser Blood |
| 83-87 | 21-30% | Lesser Blood |
| 88-91 | 31-39% | Lesser Blood |
| 92-94 | 40-55% | Archon |
| 95-96 | 56-70% | Archon |
| 97 | 71-85% | High Archon |
| 98 | 86-94% | Scion |
| 99 | 95-98% | Sovereign (Dormant) |
| 00 | 99-100% | True Sovereign (Awakened) — Campaign-defining |

### Method C: Point Buy with Narrative Cost (For Experienced Players)

Players can "buy" higher purity with narrative drawbacks:
- 0% (Blank): +1 bonus feat. You've survived this long as a nobody — you're tougher.
- 1-14% (Common): Standard character. No modifiers.
- 15-39% (Lesser Blood): -1 Constitution. Your body strains against even minor magic.
- 40-70% (Archon): Start with a powerful enemy (rival house, jealous Inquisitor, or Cult interest).
- 71-85% (High Archon): Start with 1 level of Exhaustion representing the constant political pressure.
- 86-94% (Scion): Dual-Heartbeat. You must hide your nature. -2 to Deception checks about your identity.
- 95-98% (Sovereign/Dormant): MC Arc. Dormancy Penalty (-4 all standard rolls). The DM will build the campaign tension around you. You are the hunted.
- 99-100%: Not available at character creation. This is an endgame state.

## Part 3: Age Rules Per Race

### Minimum Starting Age
A character MUST have undergone their race's **Day of Ascension** to be playable. You cannot start as a child who hasn't been tested.

| Race | Ascension Age | Minimum Starting Age | Rationale |
|------|---------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Human | 15 | 15 | Standard |
| Dracon-Kin | 12 | 12 | Earlier physical maturity due to draconic features |
| Stone-Blood | 25 | 25 | Slow maturation, underground longevity |
| Ash-Walker | 15 | 15 | Human-lineage base |
| Deep-Blood | 30 | 30 | Longest pre-Ascension period — deep cultural rituals |
| Sump-Blood | 15 | 15 | Human offshoot |
| Bone-Wrought | N/A | N/A | Created, not born. No Ascension. |
| Half-Breed | 18 | 18 | Delayed maturation from hybrid biology |

### Maximum Starting Age

A character cannot start older than approximately 2/3 of their race's average lifespan. This prevents players from starting as geriatric characters to exploit age-related purity decay (Deep-Bloods) or accumulated wealth/wisdom.

| Race | Average Lifespan | Max Starting Age | Rationale |
|------|-----------------|------------------|-----------|
| Human | 70 | 50 | Peak physical ability fades after 50 |
| Dracon-Kin | 80 | 50 | Draconic features degrade — scales flake, breath weakens |
| Stone-Blood | 350 | 200 | Middle age for Stone-Bloods is ~175 |
| Ash-Walker | 120 | 80 | Aether scarring accelerates aging |
| Deep-Blood | 750 | 500 | Peak purity window closes by 400. After 500, decay is irreversible. |
| Sump-Blood | 150 | 100 | Hard underground living shortens viable adventuring years |
| Bone-Wrought | Unlimited | No limit | Constructs don't age. A 400-year-old Bone-Wrought could be viable. |
| Half-Breed | 180 | 120 | Hybrid vigor fades |

### Aging Effects

As characters age beyond their starting maximum, these effects apply (for NPC aging or long-term campaigns):

| Age Range | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Youth (Ascension age to 1/3 lifespan) | Normal stats |
| Adult (1/3 to 2/3 lifespan) | Normal stats. This is the "adventuring prime." |
| Middle Age (2/3 to 4/5 lifespan) | -1 STR, -1 DEX, -1 CON, +1 INT, +1 WIS, +1 CHA (cumulative, applied once at transition) |
| Old Age (4/5 to end of lifespan) | -2 STR, -2 DEX, -2 CON (replaces middle age penalties), +1 INT, +1 WIS, +1 CHA (stacks with middle age). Speed reduced by 10 ft. |
| Ancient (beyond average lifespan) | DM's discretion. Typically -4 STR/DEX/CON, +2 INT/WIS, frail physique. |

### Purity Decay (Deep-Bloods Only)

Deep-Bloods are unique: their blood purity slowly degrades over centuries. A character who starts at 400 years old may have already lost significant casting ability.

**Purity Decay Rate:** After age 100, Deep-Bloods lose approximately 1% purity per 10 years.

| Starting Age | Purity Range | Effective Casting |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| 30-100 (Young) | 42-80% | Full Archon capabilities |
| 101-250 (Adult) | 22-60% | Lesser Blood to Archon |
| 251-400 (Middle Age) | 7-45% | Common to Lesser Blood |
| 401-500 (Old) | 1-20% | Blank to Common |
| 500+ (Ancient) | 0-5% | Functionally Blank |

A 500-year-old Deep-Blood who was once an 80% Archon is now a 0-5% Blank. They remember casting mighty spells. They can't anymore. This is the Deep-Blood tragedy — and a powerful character motivation.

## Part 4: Character Creation Checklist

### Session Zero Procedure

**Step 1: Race Selection**
Choose race. Note Ascension age and lifespan. This determines your age range (min to max).

**Step 2: Age Selection**
Choose an age within your race's allowed starting range. Consider:
- Younger = less experience but more potential time for the campaign
- Older = more backstory, possible purity decay (Deep-Bloods), accumulated resources
- Do NOT exceed max starting age

**Step 3: Blood Purity Determination**
Choose your purity determination method:
- Method A (Recommended): Discuss with DM. Narratively assigned.
- Method B: Roll on weighted table. Accept the result.
- Method C: Point-buy with drawbacks.

Record your purity percentage. This is permanent (except for Deep-Blood decay).

**Step 4: Caste & Social Standing**
Based on your purity, determine:
- Starting caste (Blank, Common, Lesser Blood, Archon, Scion, Sovereign)
- Legal rights (can you cast magic? own property? testify?)
- Starting wealth (nobles get GC, commoners get CF)
- Social connections (Archons know other nobles; Blanks know other laborers)

**Step 5: Class Selection**
Not all classes are available to all castes:
- **Any purity:** Vanguard (Fighter, Barbarian, Monk), Strider-Garrison (Rogue, Ranger)
- **15%+ only:** Archon Caster (Wizard, Sorcerer) — Spell Safety saves required
- **Any purity:** Ordained (Cleric, Paladin) — Church-sanctioned magic operates differently
- **Any purity:** Penitent (Warlock) — Pact magic bypasses purity... dangerously

**Step 6: Background & House Affiliation**
- Choose a background (customize standard 5e backgrounds for Solis-Grave: Laborer, Noble Scion, Inquisitor Cadet, Cult Recruit, Street Urchin/Sump-Rat, Academy Student, Merchant Guard, Core-Thief)
- High purity characters (40%+) may belong to a House (optional)
- Characters with House affiliation gain that House's Trait (see House Traits in setting)
- Blanks cannot belong to a House unless adopted/servant status

**Step 7: Ability Scores**
Standard array, point buy, or roll (DM's choice). No modifications from purity.

**Step 8: Starting Equipment**
Based on class and background. Currency based on caste:
- Blank: 1d4 x 10 Copper Farthings + class equipment
- Common: Class equipment + 2d4 x 10 Silver Scales
- Lesser Blood: Class equipment + 4d4 x 10 Silver Scales
- Archon: Class equipment + 4d4 x 10 Gold Crowns (rich)
- Scion/Sovereign: DM's discretion (likely nothing — you're in hiding or Church custody)

**Step 9: Day of Ascension Backstory**
Write 2-3 sentences about your Day of Ascension:
- WHERE were you tested? (Village square, city cathedral, Citadel testing chamber?)
- WHO administered the test? (Kind Inquisitor, cruel Blood-Judge, your own parent?)
- WHAT was the reaction? (Joy, disappointment, fear, silence, chaos?)
- This is your character's single most formative memory. Make it count.

**Step 10: Character Sheet Finalization**
- Record all stats, abilities, purity %
- Calculate Spell Safety DC: 20 - (Purity / 5)
- If 95%+: Apply Dormancy Penalty (-4 all standard rolls), Dual-Heartbeat active, Sovereign Surge (+9 hidden)
- If 0% (Blank): Note magic immunity advantage on spell saves
- Record House Trait if applicable
- Starting HP, AC, equipment

## Part 5: In-Game Purity Changes

While purity is largely fixed at Ascension, certain in-game events can change it:

| Event | Purity Change | Permanence |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| Soul-Meld Ritual (forbidden) | Fuses two souls. Result = average of both purities or random. 50% mortality rate. | Permanent |
| Aether Catastrophe Exposure | Roll 1d100. On 1-10: +1d10% (corruption). On 91-100: -1d10% (burnout). | Permanent |
| Sovereign Awakening (95%+ only) | Dormancy Penalty removed. Full purity access unlocked. | Permanent — Campaign defining moment |
| Dragon Blood Transfusion | Illegal. Roll CON save DC 20. Success: +1d20% purity (max 85%). Failure: Death from rejection. | Permanent if successful |
| Ascension Crystal Re-Test | Using a pristine crystal, re-testing is possible but illegal and dangerous. DC 15 CON save or take 3d6 Aether Burn from the crystal's feedback. | Re-read only — purity doesn't change, just gets confirmed |
| Deep-Blood Age Decay | After age 100, -1% purity per 10 years. Cannot be reversed. | Permanent and inevitable |
| God-Forged Artifact Attunement | Some legendary items can temporarily boost purity by 10-20% while attuned. This is the closest thing to "borrowing" godhood. | Temporary — lasts while attuned |
