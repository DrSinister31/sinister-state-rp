# The Sovereigns & Gods of Ourobora

## The Six Sovereign Dragons

### Ignivar — The Fire-Heart (Fire, Conquest, Magma)
**House:** Ignis | **Motto:** "Ash and Submission"
**Role in the Betrayal:** Led the assassination. Ignivar feared Nyx because the Betrayed's Void domain could unmake anything Ignivar built. He convinced Vortakarn and Tenebraxis to join him. He delivered the first strike.
**First Sin:** Burned Viridomir, the Nature God, to ash. The Sulfur Wastes are Viridomir's remains. The Church celebrates this as the founding of civilization.

### Vortakarn — The Storm-Crown (Sky, Wind, Lightning)
**House:** Vortex | **Motto:** "We Rule the Wind, We Own the Horizon"
**Role in the Betrayal:** Held Nyx down. Loyal to Ignivar out of fear rather than conviction. His guilt manifests in House Vortex's obsession with escape — they're always in the sky, always moving.
**Legacy:** The storms that wrack the northern coasts are said to be Vortakarn's guilty conscience — his winds still screaming at what he helped do.

### Obsidrath — The Law-Speaker (Judgment, Stone, Truth)
**House:** Obsidian | **Motto:** "Law Bends to the Pure"
**Role in the Betrayal:** Refused to participate, but stayed silent. Knew it was murder. Said nothing. His silence became House Obsidian's shame — they overcompensate with rigid law and purity enforcement. If you can judge everyone else harshly enough, nobody looks at your own guilt.
**Legacy:** The first Blood Crystal was made from Obsidrath's tears.

### Tenebraxis — The Flesh-Shaper (Mutation, Darkness, Flesh)
**House:** Tenebris | **Motto:** "Truth Bleeds in the Dark"
**Role in the Betrayal:** Performed the killing blow. The only one who could. His domain over flesh was the only power that could bind Void in a body. He scattered Nyx's essence across mortal bloodlines — not out of mercy, but because he couldn't bear to look at what he'd done. The scattering was a desperate, horrified attempt to undo the killing.
**Legacy:** House Tenebris's flesh-fusion experiments are a subconscious attempt to reverse-engineer what Tenebraxis did — to learn how to un-murder a god.

### Ferrumal — The Iron-Bound (Shield, Endurance, Earth)
**House:** Ferrum | **Motto:** "Iron Endures the Storm"
**Role in the Betrayal:** Voted no. Was outnumbered and exiled to the borderlands before the killing. Arrived too late to stop it. Found only blood and silence. He never spoke again. His shield, still planted in the northern wastes, is said to weep rust.
**Legacy:** House Ferrum guards the borderlands not from monsters, but from the guilt of what their founder couldn't prevent. Every Ferrum soldier who drops to 1 HP instead of dying is channeling Ferrumal's defiance.

### Nyx — The Betrayed, The Sleeper, The Tail (Void, Time, Cycles)
**House:** None — erased from history.
**Role in the Betrayal:** The youngest. The cycle-keeper. His domain was Void — not emptiness, but the space between things. The end that makes beginnings possible. The other five feared him because he could UNMAKE what they built. The ouroboros is his symbol: the serpent eating its tail, the cycle he governed.
**How He Died:** Ignivar struck first (fire). Vortakarn held him (storm). Obsidrath watched (silence). Tenebraxis delivered the killing blow (flesh). Ferrumal arrived too late (iron). Nyx's essence was scattered across mortal bloodlines — fragments of Void seeded through humanity.
**The Reincarnation:** The fragments are slowly recombining. One person in each generation carries the densest concentration. They appear Blank (0%) on Blood Crystals because Void registers as "nothing." They don't know what they are. They won't know until the fragments cohere enough to speak — or until a Sovereign Dragon recognizes them.

## The Gods

### Viridomir — The Wild-King (Nature, Beasts, Seasons, Forests)
**Status:** DEAD. Burned by Ignivar at the dawn of the Age of Crowns.
**Remains:**
- **Flesh → The Sulfur Wastes:** His body burned. The ash still smolders.
- **Spine → The Northern Mountain Range:** His skeleton became the peaks.
- **Tears → The Sump-Waters:** His grief still weeps through underground channels.
- **Heart → Unknown:** The Cult believes his heart still beats somewhere. Finding it would restore him.
**Connection to Nyx:** Viridomir and Nyx were allies — the god of wild nature and the god of cycles. The Wheel and the Wild. When Ignivar burned Viridomir, Nyx grieved. The other five saw his grief as weakness. It was the first crack that led to the betrayal.
**Why Beasts Submit:** Animals still remember Viridomir. When they encounter Nyx's soul-vessel, they sense their fallen king's last ally. They submit in reverence and hope. "Perhaps this one will restore the wild."

## The Church of the Five Skulls

### Official Doctrine
- Five Sovereign Dragons willingly sacrificed their physical forms to anchor reality.
- Ignivar burned the beast-god Viridomir, freeing civilization from chaos. **First Great Deed.**
- The five watch over humanity from the Aether. Their skulls are the pillars of existence.
- There has NEVER been a Sixth Sovereign. Claiming otherwise is highest heresy.
- Blood Purity is divine will. Your percentage is what the Five decided you deserve.

### What They Know (And Suppress)
- Nyx existed. They murdered him. They erased his name from every record.
- The "Five Skulls" doctrine was invented to cover the murder — five skulls, not six.
- Some Inquisitors carry fragments of true history. These are the ones who stare too long at Blank recruits, knowing SOMETHING is wrong but unable to articulate it.
- The Church's greatest fear: the Sixth returns. Every heresy purge, every Inquisitorial Smite, is ultimately about preventing this.

## The Cult of the Sixth

### What They Believe
- The five DID NOT sacrifice themselves willingly. Four murdered the Sixth. Ferrumal tried to stop it.
- Nyx did not truly die. His essence was scattered and is slowly recombining.
- One day, the fragments will coalesce in a vessel. The Betrayed will walk again.
- When he returns, the ouroboros will close. The cycle will resume. The five crowns will break.

### Their Signs
- The "Dig Deep" work chant encodes the prophecy in manual labor terms.
- Void-coded individuals (appear Blank, feel wrong to Church NPCs) are watched with hope.
- Ancient dragon bone fragments that hum near certain people — Cult "detectors."
- Every natural disaster is read as Nyx stirring. Every stillbirth as a potential vessel that failed.

### The Prophecy (Full Text)
"Five needles pierced the tongue of the deep,
Five thieves sowed what they could not reap.
They drank the oil, they burned the bone,
And built a glass cage to mimic the throne.
But the Tail shall meet the swallowing Maw,
When the five crowns break under older law.
The sparkless child is a lie of the skin,
A forgotten god folded deep within.
No spark is shown, yet the furnace shall weep,
When a hand reaches down where the blind wyrms creep.
A twin-beat shall wake in the sump-water black,
The great wheel turns and it will not turn back.
For the flesh is the shroud where the Sovereign sleeps,
And the soul is the flame that the charcoal keeps.
The clay shall dissolve as the scales unfold,
And Ouroboros consumes what the five bought and sold."
